WATER CONSERVATION AND SUSTAINABLE FARMING PRACTICES

PER DROP MORE CROP - PRADHAN MANTRI KRISHI SINCHAI YOJANA (PMKSY)

Mission: To achieve convergence of investments in irrigation sector, enhance water use efficiency, end-to-end solutions for source creation, distribution network, field application and extension activities.

DRIP IRRIGATION BENEFITS:
- Water savings: 30-50% compared to flood irrigation
- Yield increase: 20-50% due to precise water delivery
- Fertilizer savings: 25-30% through fertigation
- Labour savings: 70-80%
- Suitable for: Fruits, vegetables, sugarcane, cotton, maize

SPRINKLER IRRIGATION:
- Water savings: 25-35% compared to flood irrigation
- Suitable for: Wheat, pulses, oilseeds, fodder crops
- Mobile sprinklers available for small farmers on rental basis

RAINWATER HARVESTING:
- Farm ponds for storing rainwater
- Government subsidy: Rs 90,000 per farm pond (45m x 30m x 3m)
- Useful in rainfed areas with 400-600mm annual rainfall

CROP ROTATION GUIDELINES FOR SOIL HEALTH:

1. Rice - Wheat - Moong (North India standard rotation)
   Benefits: Breaks pest cycles, improves nitrogen fixation with moong

2. Cotton - Wheat - Groundnut (Central/West India)
   Benefits: Reduces soil erosion, improves soil organic matter

3. Sugarcane - Ratoon - Pulse (South India)
   Benefits: Reduces replanting cost, adds nitrogen

4. Vegetable - Pulse - Cereal (All regions)
   Benefits: Maximum income, soil health maintenance

AVOID THESE PRACTICES:
- Growing same crop consecutively for more than 2 seasons
- Burning crop residue (contributes to pollution, destroys soil microbes)
- Excessive use of urea without balancing P and K
- Waterlogging (leads to root diseases and methane emissions)

SOIL ORGANIC MATTER IMPROVEMENT:
- Add farmyard manure: 10-15 tonnes/hectare
- Green manuring with Sesbania, Sunhemp: 6-8 weeks before transplanting
- Vermicompost application: 5 tonnes/hectare
- Biofertilizers: Rhizobium for pulses, Azotobacter for cereals (free from Krishi Vigyan Kendras)

INDICATORS OF GOOD SOIL HEALTH:
- pH between 6.0 and 7.5
- Organic carbon > 0.5%
- Good drainage and aeration
- Earthworm presence (5-8 per sq ft)
- No salt encrustation on surface

For soil testing and recommendations contact your nearest:
- Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK)
- State Agriculture Department soil testing lab
- Private certified soil testing labs
