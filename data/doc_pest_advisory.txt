INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) GUIDELINES FOR MAJOR CROPS

RICE:
Common Pests: Brown Plant Hopper (BPH), Stem Borer, Leaf Folder
Symptoms: Deadheart in young plants, whiteear in older plants, hopper burn
Management: 
- Use resistant varieties (MTU 7029, Swarna Sub1)
- Install light traps to monitor moth populations
- Apply neem-based pesticides in early stages
- Maintain 2.5 cm water level to drown eggs
- Chemical: Chlorpyrifos 20EC at 1.5 L/ha if infestation exceeds ETL

WHEAT:
Common Diseases: Yellow Rust, Brown Rust, Loose Smut
Symptoms: Yellow/orange powder on leaves, grain replacement by black spore mass
Management:
- Use certified disease-free seeds
- Seed treatment with Vitavax Power 2.5g/kg seed
- Foliar spray of Propiconazole 25EC at 0.1% at flag leaf stage

COTTON:
Common Pests: Bollworms (American, Spotted, Pink), Whitefly, Aphids
Symptoms: Square/boll shedding, curling of leaves, sticky leaves
Management:
- Use Bt cotton varieties for bollworm management
- Install pheromone traps (5/acre for bollworm monitoring)
- Spray neem oil 3% + garlic extract for early infestation
- Chemical: Spinosad 45SC at 0.05% for bollworms

VEGETABLES (Tomato, Chilli, Brinjal):
Common Issues: Early Blight, Late Blight, Fruit Borer, Leaf Curl Virus
Management:
- Use virus-resistant varieties
- Install yellow sticky traps for whitefly/aphid monitoring
- Copper fungicide for fungal diseases
- Remove and destroy infected plants immediately

PULSES (Dal crops):
Common Pests: Aphids, Thrips, Pod Borer, Stem Fly
Management:
- Seed treatment with Imidacloprid 70WS
- Spray Profenofos 50EC for pod borer
- Inter-cropping with cereal crops reduces pest incidence

GENERAL ADVISORY:
- Always monitor fields twice weekly during critical growth stages
- Follow label instructions for all pesticide applications
- Maintain spray records for each application
- Use personal protective equipment (PPE) during spraying
- Follow pre-harvest interval (PHI) strictly

WARNING SIGNS REQUIRING IMMEDIATE ACTION:
- More than 20% leaf area damaged
- More than 5% infestation in pulse crops
- Sudden wilting of plants in patches
- Presence of locusts or armyworms (report immediately to district agriculture office)
